Hand sanitizer started flying off the shelves when Covid-19 made it's American debut last year. While it is possible that all of those bottles were recycled, I have a pretty good feeling the majority of them ended up in landfills. Fortunately, hand sanitizer is ridiculously easy to make at home, so you don't have to worry about wasting any more plastic bottles for the sake of cleanliness.

According to the CDC sanitizers with at least 60% alcohol are effective for getting rid of germs.

What you'll need:

  • 2/3 cup rubbing alcohol
  • 1/3 aloe vera gel. In this tutorial I'll take the gel from my aloe vera plant, but you can also use bottled aloe vera gel.
  • (Optional) Essential Oil. I chose tea tree oil because it has antibacterial properties and I like the smell
  • Small bottle with pump or spray. I used a small hand sanitizer bottle that I already had.
  • Funnel for pouring
