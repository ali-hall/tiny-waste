  1. Paper consumption in the United States for the last 20 years has increased from 92 million tons to 208 million, a growth of 126%. ~ Record Nations
  2. Paper and paperboard (cardboard) materials make up the largest component of municipal solid waste (MSW). ~ EPA
  3. In 2018, paper made up 11.8% of landfills. ~ EPA
  4. Paper and paperboad is the most recyclyed material. ~ EPA
  5. The average American uses seven trees a year in paper, wood, and other products made from trees. This amounts to about 2,000,000,000 trees per year! University of Southern Indiana
  6. The average office worker uses 10,000 sheets of copy paper each year. Record Nations
  7. 45% of paper printed in offices ends up trashed by the end of the day. Road Runner
  8. Paper production is responsible for 14% of deforastation. ~ Live Science
  9. Deforestation is the second leading cause of climate change (the first is the burning of fossil fuels). ~ Food and Agricultural Organization
