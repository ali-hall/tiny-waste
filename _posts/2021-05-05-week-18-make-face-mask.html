Sheet masks are my ultimate luxury pampering item. My sister and I have girls' nights in that revolve around those goopy little XXX. This week I was horrified to discover that my beloved sheet masks are not only packaged in plastic, but the masks themselves are plastic. So, even though they are typically only a few dollars a pop and rarely bigger than a XXX, they add up in both monetary and landfill-bound waste.

Sheet mask recipes:

Rice Water Brightening Mask

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of rice
  • 1 cup water

Rice

Green Tea

  • Green Tea (ditch the plastic tea bag and brew your loose leaf tea in a tea ball)

Both oral consumption and topical application of green tea protect against inflammation. To use it for your sheet mask, steep in boiling water for 5 minutes, let cool, and add your reusable mask. Place the tea and mask in a refrigerator for 20-30 minutes then apply the mask to your face for 20-30 minutes.

Cucumber Mint Refreshing Mask

  • 1/2 cucumber
  • Peppermint tea (ditch the plastic tea bag and brew your loose leaf tea in a tea ball)

What you'll need to make the mask:

  • >Natural fabric, about the size of a sheet of paper. Think old bed sheet, stained blouse, or fabric remnant.
  • Chalk, pencil, or marker to trace pattern
  • Scissors
  • Sewing machine or serger for finishing edges
  • Free sheet mask pattern. Click here to download.
