Sometimes take out is a necessity, and while some containers are recyclable, not all are. For instance salads often come in #6 plastics, but those aren't always accepted in curbside recycling. Instead of sending thosee #6 plastics to a landfill, you can make cute, on-of-a-kind jewelry!

What You'll Need:

  • #6 Plastics - only clear #6 will work, like clamshells
  • Sharpies - you can use pencil colors too, but you'll have to sand the plastic first
  • Scissors
  • Scrap paper for making stencil
  • Hole Punch - I used scissors, but if you have a hole punch it will make things cleaner and easier
  • Jewelry findings
  • Oven

Let's Make Shrinkie Dink Earrings!

  1. 1. Grab a clear #6 plastic container. I see them used most for salads and berries.

  2. 2. Cut out the flat areas of your clamshell. I had one from the top and one from the bottom.

  3. 3. Use a scrap piece of paper to draw a stencil that is the appropriate size for your plastic pieces.

  4. 4. Trace the stencil onto your plastic piece.

  5. 5. Use sharpies to color in your image. You can also use pencil colors, but you'll have to sand the plastic first.

  6. 6. Cut out the design you wish to shrink.

  7. 7. Use a hole punch (or scissors) to put a hole in the plastic, wherever you would like add the jewelry findings. Make sure to put it no more that a quartere of an inch from the edge.

  8. 8. Preheat your oven to 325°. Put your plastic on a piece of scrap foil, marker side up, and bake for 3 minutes. Let cool before handling.

  9. 9. Add jewelry findings to complete I made earrings, but you could make keychains, charm bracelets, necklaces... anything you want!
