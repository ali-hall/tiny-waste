Now that I've gotten a handle on what can go into my curbside recycling bins, it's time to find a way to recycle everything else. Here are a few things that come up fairly frequently in my house: Electronic waste: Only 10% of global e-waste gets recycled, while the rest of it ends up getting landfilled or incinerated. E-waste is made of all sorts of harmful chemicals, so if it is mishandled during disposal, these chemicals end up in our soil, water, and air. Best Buy takes loads of electronic waste. https://www.bestbuy.com/site/services/recycling/pcmcat149900050025.c?id=pcmcat149900050025 Running Shoes: Old kicks can be donated to an organization like Soles4Souls, which gives gently used shoes to people in poverty. Or, they can be dropped off at any Nike location, where they will be recycling into new material. https://www.nike.com/help/a/recycle-shoes Batteries: Home Depot and Lowes both recycle batteries. In most locations, you can drop them off in store. Everything else: Just about anything you can think of can be recycled through Terracycle. https://www.terracycle.com/en-US/ Simply order a box, fill it up and send it back.
